# StreamQueue Support

StreamQueue is a shared streaming queue and Roku remote utility for households.

## Getting Help

For support, questions, bug reports, or privacy requests, contact:

support@sdudley.com

When reporting an issue, please include:

- Your iPhone model and iOS version.
- Whether you are on your home Wi-Fi network.
- Your Roku model or Roku TV model, if known.
- What you were trying to do.
- Any error message shown in StreamQueue.

## Roku Setup

For StreamQueue to find and control your Roku, your phone and Roku must be on the same Wi-Fi network.

On your Roku, make sure network control is enabled:

**Settings -> System -> Advanced system settings -> Control by mobile apps -> Network access -> Enabled**

If StreamQueue cannot find your Roku:

- Confirm your phone is connected to the same Wi-Fi network as the Roku.
- Confirm Roku Network Access is enabled.
- Open StreamQueue Settings and tap **Scan for Roku devices**.
- Restart the Roku if it still does not appear.
- Check whether your router blocks local device discovery or client-to-client communication.

## Watch Now

The **Watch Now** button launches a streaming app on your selected Roku and, when supported by that app, opens the selected title directly.

Deep linking support varies by streaming service. Some apps can open the exact movie, show, season, or episode. Other apps may only open the streaming app, and you may need to navigate manually from there.

## Remote

The StreamQueue remote works only when your phone is on the same local network as the selected Roku.

If remote commands do not work:

- Confirm you are on home Wi-Fi.
- Confirm the correct Roku is selected in Settings.
- Re-scan for Roku devices.
- Make sure the Roku is powered on and reachable from the network.

## Account and Household Sharing

StreamQueue can sync a shared queue across household members. Invite codes expire after 15 minutes. If a code expires, generate a new one from Settings.
